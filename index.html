📖 Finance Basics

📈 What is a stock?

A stock is a small piece of ownership in a company. When you buy Apple stock (AAPL), you own a tiny fraction of Apple. If Apple does well and grows, your piece becomes worth more. If it struggles, your piece is worth less.

📊 What is NASDAQ / S&P 500 / Dow?

NASDAQ 100 — tracks the top 100 tech companies like Apple, Nvidia, Google. If tech is doing well, NASDAQ goes up.

S&P 500 — tracks the top 500 US companies across ALL industries. It's the most watched number — it tells you how the US economy is doing overall.

Dow Jones — tracks just 30 very large, traditional companies like Disney, Boeing, Coca-Cola. Older and less tech-focused than the others.

🟢🔴 What do the colors mean?

Green / +% = the price went UP today. Good if you own it.

Red / -% = the price went DOWN today. If you own it, it's worth less right now.

Small daily moves (+/- 1-2%) are totally normal. Big moves (+/- 5%+) usually mean big news happened.

😨 What is Fear vs Greed?

This measures the overall mood of investors right now, based on news.

Fear / Panic — people are scared and selling. Prices drop. This can actually be a buying opportunity.

Greed / Hype — everyone is excited and buying. Prices go up. Be careful — this can mean things are overpriced.

Famous quote: "Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful." — Warren Buffett

🏭 What are sectors / industries?

The stock market is divided into groups of similar companies. If oil prices rise, Energy stocks go up. If people are sick, Healthcare stocks move. Watching sectors tells you which parts of the economy are doing well or badly today.

📉 Why does news affect stocks?

Stock prices are driven by what investors think a company's future will look like. Good news (big profits, new product, deal won) = investors get excited = price goes up. Bad news (losses, scandal, competition) = investors get scared = price drops. Sometimes even global news (wars, interest rate changes, elections) can move the whole market.

💡 What is an ETF?

An ETF (Exchange Traded Fund) is like a basket of stocks. Instead of buying one company, you buy a little bit of many companies at once. QQQ is an ETF that holds all 100 NASDAQ companies. If you buy QQQ, you're basically investing in all of them at the same time. Less risk than buying just one stock.
